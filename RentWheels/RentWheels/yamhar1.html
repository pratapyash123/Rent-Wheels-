Yamaha R1 Motorcycle

The Yamaha YZF-R1 is a supersport motorcycle that was first introduced in 1997

Engine

The R1 has a 998cc, liquid-cooled, inline 4-cylinder engine that produces 197 horsepower at 13,500 rpm and 112.4 Nm of torque at 11,500 rpm.

Transmission

The R1 has a 6-speed transmission system with multiplate assist and slipper clutch.

Electronics

The R1 has a number of electronic features, including a Traction Control System (TCS), Slide Control System (SCS), anti-wheelie Lift Control System (LIF), linked antilock brakes, a Launch Control System (LCS), and a Quick Shift System (QSS).

Performance

The R1 can go from 0 to 100 km/h in 2.7 seconds and has a top speed of 299 km/h.

Dimensions

The R1 is 2055 mm long, 691 mm wide, and 1150 mm high, with a ground clearance of 130 mm and a wheelbase of 1404 mm.

Models

There are multiple models of the R1, including the R1M, which has more expensive components, and the R1S, which is a lower-spec model
